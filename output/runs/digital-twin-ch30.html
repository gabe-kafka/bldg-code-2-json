30.1.1 Building Types
text_block This chapter applies to the determination of wind pressures on components and cladding (C&C) on buildings.
text_block Part 1 is applicable to an enclosed, partially enclosed, or partially open
text_block Low-rise building (see de fi nition in Section 26.2); or
→ ASCE7-22-26.2-H1
text_block Building with h ≤ 60 ft (18.3 m).
text_block The building has a fl at roof, gable roof, multispan gable roof, hip roof, monoslope roof, stepped roof, or sawtooth roof, and the wind pressures are calculated from a wind pressure equation.
text_block Part 2 is applicable to an enclosed, partially enclosed, or partially open
text_block Building with h > 60 ft (18.3 m).
text_block The building has a fl at roof, pitched roof, gable roof, hip roof, mansard roof, arched roof, or domed roof, and the wind pressures are calculated from a wind pressure equation.
text_block Part 3 is applicable to an open building of all heights that has a pitched free roof, monoslope free roof, or troughed free roof.
text_block Part 4 is applicable to building appurtenances such as roof overhangs, parapets, and rooftop equipment.
text_block Part 5 is applicable to non-building structures -circular bins, silos, and tanks; rooftop solar panels and roof pavers.
text_block Circular bins, silos, and tanks with h ≤ 120 ft (38.6 m);
text_block Rooftop solar panels: Buildings of all heights with fl at roofs or gable or hip roofs with roof slopes less than or equal to 7 degrees; and
text_block Roof pavers: Buildings of all heights with roof slopes less than or equal to 7 degrees.
30.1.2 Conditions
text_block A building that has design wind loads determined in accordance with this chapter shall comply with all of the following conditions:
text_block The building is a regular-shaped building as de fi ned in Section 26.2; and
→ ASCE7-22-26.2-H1
text_block The building does not have response characteristics that make it subject to across-wind loading, vortex shedding, or instability caused by galloping or fl utter; nor does it have a site location for which channeling effects or buffeting in the wake of upwind obstructions warrant special consideration.
